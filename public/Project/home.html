Product Description:

Television
Television, commonly known as TV, is an electronic device used for receiving and displaying broadcast signals, typically consisting of audio and video content. Modern TVs come in various types, including LED, OLED, and QLED, offering high-definition and smart features. Some of the main internal parts are listed on the side.
The display panel is the screen that produces images using technologies like LCD, LED, OLED, or QLED. The motherboard or mainboard is the central circuit board that houses the processor, memory, and other essential components, coordinating the TV's functions. The CPU processes instructions, controls tasks, and manages overall functionality, contributing to image rendering and smart features. The PSU converts electrical power from an outlet into a form suitable for the TV, providing the necessary energy to operate. The audio system includes speakers and components responsible for producing sound to accompany the video, providing a complete multimedia experience.
Laptop
Laptop is a portable computer designed for mobility and convenience. It typically includes a keyboard, display screen, and a built-in battery, allowing users to work or play on the go. Some of their main internal parts are listed on the side.
The CPU is the brain of the laptop, executing instructions and handling computations necessary for various tasks. RAM is volatile memory used for temporarily storing data that the CPU needs for quick access, contributing to smooth multitasking. Storage devices, such as hard disk drives (HDD) or solid-state drives (SSD), store the laptop's operating system, applications, and user data. The GPU is responsible for rendering graphics and enhancing the visual performance of the laptop, crucial for gaming and multimedia applications. The battery provides power for the laptop when not connected to an external power source, enabling portability and uninterrupted usage.
Smartphone
Smartphone is a handheld communication device with advanced features, including calling, texting, internet browsing, and app support. It combines a mobile phone with computing capabilities, providing a versatile tool for communication and productivity. Some of their main internal parts are listed on the side.
The SoC integrates the central processing unit (CPU), graphics processing unit (GPU), and other components, enabling the execution of tasks, running applications, and ensuring overall device performance. A rechargeable battery supplies power to the smartphone, allowing for extended usage. The battery capacity determines the device's runtime, and advancements in battery technology enhance overall battery life. The display screen serves as the primary interface, providing a visual platform for user interaction with apps, multimedia, and the operating system. Modern smartphones feature various display technologies like OLED and AMOLED for vibrant visuals. Smartphones incorporate advanced camera systems, including front and rear cameras with high-resolution sensors and multiple lenses. These cameras capture photos, record videos, and support features like portrait mode and night mode. Internal storage, usually in the form of flash memory, stores the smartphone's operating system, installed apps, and user data. It facilitates quick access to applications and ensures seamless performance.
